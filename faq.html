Eye care, including medical examinations and treatments, is personalized for each individual. To determine the most appropriate approach for your needs, it is advisable to consult your optometrist. In general, the Canadian Association of Optometrists provides the following recommendations for the frequency of eye exams for individuals at low risk of vision loss:
Infants and Toddlers (Birth to 24 months): The first eye examination should be scheduled between 6 and 9 months of age.
Preschool Children (2 to 5 years): Annual eye examinations are recommended.
Age Children (6 to 19 years): Annual eye examinations are recommended.
Adults (20 to 64 years): Eye examinations should be scheduled every 2 years.
Adults (65 years or older): Annual eye examinations are recommended.
