Meet our optometrists

Dr. Grace Johnson

Originally from Toronto, Dr. Grace Johnson moved to Regina in 2011 after graduating from the New England College of Optometry in Boston.

Read more

She completed internships at the Veteran’s Affairs Hospitals in Manchester and Boston, then returned to Toronto and trained at the nationally renowned Vision Institute of Canada. Outside of work, Dr. Johnson is enjoying the adventure of being a new parent and spending time with her family. In January 2016, Dr. Johnson joined Prairie Vision and is currently accepting new patients.

Dr. Wyatt Nelson

Dr. Nelson received his Bachelor of Science and Doctor of Optometry degree in 1992. Since moving to Regina, Dr. Nelson has been providing comprehensive vision care in the Regina community.

Read more

Dr. Nelson is a true sports fan and has been volunteering in the area of sports vision with the Special Olympics world games and locally with the Regina Pats, the Rough Riders and Renegades. He is a member of the International Sports Vision Academy and the American Optometric Association Sports vision section. Dr. Nelson coaches and sponsors the South-end Minor Hockey association and South Regina Little League teams. He is a guest lecturer at the University of Waterloo in the area of sports vision and concussion rehabilitation.

Xavier Best

Dr. Best was born in Montréal and grew up in Cornwall, Ontario. He earned his Honours Bachelor of Science in Biomedical Science at the University of Ottawa in 2017, then his Doctorate of Optometry at the Massachusetts College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences in the spring of 2021.

Read more

After rotating through several community health centers and high-volume private practices in the United States, he began working as an independent Optometrist in Canada in 2021. Dr. Best is now pleased to be a member of Prairie Eyes community in Regina. In his free time he enjoys playing and recording music, as well as partaking in winter sports such as hockey and skiing.
